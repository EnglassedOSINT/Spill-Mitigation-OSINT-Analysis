# OSINT Assessment: Industrial Spill Cleanup Sorbents (2026)
**Project Lead:** Yvette English, Founder & CEO, EM Analytics  
**Objective:** A primary-source verified comparative analysis of leading industrial absorbents across lifecycle cost, safety, and encapsulation efficacy.

## ⚠️ Executive Summary
This intelligence brief evaluates seven major spill mitigation products to transform fragmented manufacturer claims into actionable procurement intelligence. Through rigorous open-source research, **NviroClean** emerged as the superior all-around performer, while traditional clay (**Oil-Dri**) was identified as a high-risk, low-efficiency outlier.

## 🔍 Methodology & Tradecraft
To ensure reproducibility and objective results, the following OSINT workflow was utilized:
* **Primary Source Verification:** Cross-referenced marketing claims against 2026 Safety Data Sheets (SDS/MSDS) and NSF International certification records.
* **Relational Comparison Framework:** Used Python and SQL-style queries to normalize over 50 data points per product.
* **Weighted Scoring Model:** Products were evaluated on a 1–10 scale across five dimensions: 
    * Solid Ground/Eco-Land 
    * Water/Aquatic Performance 
    * Mass-Scale Suitability 
    * Safety (SDS Hazard Levels) 
    * Price Efficiency (Lifecycle cost per gallon absorbed)

## 💡 Key Findings
* **Top Performer:** **NviroClean** demonstrates superior permanent encapsulation, holding fluids under extreme pressure (up to 80,000 lbs) without leaching.
* **Environmental Risk:** Clay-based products like **Oil-Dri** present significant health risks due to crystalline silica content and higher disposal burdens.
* **Best for Water:** **PIG** and **Sphag Sorb** remain the high-efficiency standards for rapid aquatic containment.
